A

Ad Valorem

A Latin phrase meaning "according to value." Custom duties are often calculated ad valorem, based on the value of the goods being imported.

Air Waybill (AWB)

A non-negotiable document issued by an airline which serves as a receipt for the shipper and as a contract of carriage between the shipper and the carrier.

B

Bill of Lading (B/L)

Crucial Term

A legal document between a shipper and a carrier that details the type, quantity, and destination of the goods being carried. It also serves as a shipment receipt when the carrier delivers the goods at a predetermined destination.

Bonded Warehouse

A secure facility supervised by customs authorities where dutiable goods may be stored, manipulated, or undergo manufacturing operations without payment of duty.
